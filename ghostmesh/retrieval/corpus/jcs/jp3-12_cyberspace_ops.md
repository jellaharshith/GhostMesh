JP 3-12 establishes that cyberspace operations are conducted across three layers: physical (hardware, infrastructure), logical (software, protocols), and persona (user identity). Commanders must assess adversary presence at all three layers before initiating defensive cyber operations (DCO).

DCO-Internal Defensive Measures (DCO-IDM) are authorized within the commander's own networks. Offensive Cyberspace Operations (OCO) require SecDef authorization. The distinction governs response posture: isolate and hunt are DCO-IDM authorities; active counter-intrusion into adversary infrastructure requires Title 10 authority.

Key principle: hunt-forward operations gain commander visibility and create conditions to deny adversary use of malicious code. Persistent engagement — maintaining an active posture rather than waiting for adversary action — is preferred over reactive response. Detection of adversary pre-positioning should trigger immediate DCO-IDM and escalation to CISA for critical infrastructure sectors.

Posture tiers align to adversary kill-chain stage: reconnaissance → deny access; initial access → isolate and evict; persistence → hunt and restore; pre-impact staging → emergency protective measures and notification to sector partners.
