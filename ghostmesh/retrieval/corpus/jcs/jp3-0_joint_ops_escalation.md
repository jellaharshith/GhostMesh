JP 3-0 defines six operational phases: Shape (0), Deter (I), Seize Initiative (II), Dominate (III), Stabilize (IV), Enable Civil Authority (V). In contested cyberspace, most defensive operations occur in the transition between Phase 0 and Phase II.

Escalation management is a commander responsibility. The escalation control principle requires commanders to assess whether actions — including defensive cyber actions — will be perceived as escalatory by the adversary. Isolation of adversary-controlled hosts may prompt accelerated lateral movement; credential rotation may trigger session token replay attacks in the adversary's escalation calculus.

Phased response logic: Phase 0 (adversary reconnaissance confirmed) → increase monitoring, deploy deception. Phase I (initial access detected) → isolate, patch, rotate credentials. Phase II (adversary pre-positioning for impact) → restore, evacuate high-value assets, notify sector CISA. Escalation to Phase III (active disruption) requires national authority.

Attribution before action: JP 3-0 requires reasonable attribution confidence before offensive response options are presented to the National Command Authority. Premature attribution creates diplomatic risk.
