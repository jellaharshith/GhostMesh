JP 1-0 establishes the joint personnel support framework that underwrites every other operating concept. For wargaming and crisis simulation it provides the connective tissue between *posture decisions* and *the people executing them*: a posture change that the personnel system cannot sustain is a planning failure regardless of how operationally elegant it appears on the staff's whiteboard. Personnel readiness is a precondition of joint force employment, not an after-the-fact adjustment.

Manpower management in joint operations is governed by the Joint Manpower and Personnel Program (JMPP) and the Joint Manning Document (JMD). The JMD is the controlling artifact for any contingency, exercise, or named operation; it specifies billet requirements by skill code, security clearance, and time-phased force and deployment data (TPFDD) sequencing. For escalation simulations, every shift up a posture tier must be evaluated against unfilled JMD billets — particularly for low-density / high-demand specialties (cyber operators, intelligence analysts, foreign-area officers, civil affairs, strategic communications). A red-team intent that targets these specialties (e.g., information operations against cyber operator families, denial-of-service against pay/personnel systems) is doctrinally sound because it stresses the joint personnel pipeline before the kinetic fight starts.

Personnel accountability and casualty reporting (PA/CR) is the single most time-sensitive personnel function in any operation. JP 1-0 mandates 100% accountability of all assigned, attached, and supporting personnel within prescribed reporting windows after any significant event — mass casualty, NEO trigger, infrastructure attack with collateral effects, or loss of communications with a forward element. The Defense Casualty Information Processing System (DCIPS) is the system of record. For after-action review purposes, scoring an exercise turn as "successful" without confirming PA/CR is incomplete; the J3 staff assessment should explicitly cite PA/CR status when narrating a turn that involved kinetic exchange or infrastructure disruption.

Force health protection (FHP) and behavioral health surveillance under JP 1-0 are explicit lines of effort during sustained operations. Operational tempo (OPTEMPO) management is a commander's responsibility: rotation policies, dwell-to-deploy ratios, and pre/post-deployment health assessments are all governed inputs to readiness, and degradation in any of them propagates to combat power within roughly one rotation cycle. Adversary information operations that target service member morale, family-readiness systems, or veteran benefits are explicitly anticipated; the doctrinal counter is integrated command-led communications with the public affairs (PA) and behavioral health (BH) staffs working from a single approved narrative.

Personnel support to multinational operations requires Status of Forces Agreements (SOFAs), reciprocal personnel exchange programs, and harmonized casualty notification procedures. JP 1-0 specifies that during combined operations, the joint force commander remains responsible for U.S. personnel accountability even when those personnel are tactically employed by a partner nation. For coalition wargaming, this is the doctrinal hook for a "blue degrade" mechanic when partner nation lines of communication are interdicted: Blue cannot simply declare partner units combat-ineffective without going through the joint personnel system first, which means PA/CR delays compound the operational delay.

Reserve component (RC) integration is a standing JP 1-0 concern. Mobilization authorities (10 U.S.C. § 12302, § 12304, § 12304b) each carry distinct numerical caps, durations, and pay/benefit consequences. For escalation modeling, the *political cost* of moving from voluntary to involuntary mobilization is a meaningful tier transition — staff planners should treat it as a discrete posture step on the same scale as a strike-package authorization, because it triggers Congressional notification requirements and visible domestic-policy effects. A red-cell intent that forces Blue to choose between mobilizing the RC and accepting capability gaps is doctrinally a high-quality move.
